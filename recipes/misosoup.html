Carbonara Pasta Recipe

Description

ugh... miso soup. you know when you go to a japanese restaurant and they come out with miso soup before your appetizers + ontrays? tbh, that's my favorite part. miso soup is calming, delightful, and heartwarming. it's also very easy to make.

Ingredients

  • Dashi packet (Japanese soup stock)
  • 6 ounces thickly sliced guanciale, pancetta, or slab bacon, cut into 1/4-inch (6-mm) cubes
  • Miso Paste
  • Tofu
  • Dried wakame seaweed
  • Green onions (chopped)

Steps

  1. Make Dashi: Just like brewing tea, you steep the packet in hot water for a couple of minutes, and you’re left with a flavorful broth that’s surprisingly close to homemade.
  2. Add Miso Paste: When you add miso paste, turn off the heat before adding miso paste to avoid accidentally boiling the soup. Always dissolve the miso paste in a ladle or separate bowl—never add it directly to the soup—to avoid big clumps left in the soup.
  3. Add Tofu to the Soup: To avoid breaking the tofu, always add it after dissolving the miso paste. This helps keep the tofu intact and ensures a smoother texture in your soup. Since tofu doesn’t require cooking, you just need to warm it up. For miso soup, cut tofu into ½-inch (1.3 cm) cubes.
  4. Add Dried Wakame and Green Onion: Along with tofu, add dried wakame and green onions at the same time. Serve miso soup immediately while it’s warm.
